# Fixed Force Rosters (Tables of Organization and Equipment)
*Dropfleet Legionaires — authoritative battalion rosters*
*Last updated: 2026-03-20*

---

## Design Principles

1. **No builder.** Players select a battalion type and receive a fixed, doctrine-correct roster. You command what you're assigned.
2. **1980s–90s baseline.** Starting equipment is deliberately mid-tier — late Cold War Terran Federation standard issue. This leaves room for downgrades (attrition replacements with cheaper gear) and upgrades (earned through campaign progress).
3. **Roster divergence comes from play.** Two Armored battalions that started identical will look different by week two because of different casualty patterns and replacement choices (see REPLACEMENT_AND_REINFORCEMENT.md).
4. **Unit counts are realistic.** An armored company has 14 tanks. A rifle company has ~40 personnel in squads. These are not balanced against each other by count — they are balanced by mission design, replacement cost, and terrain.

## Comparison Summary

| Battalion Type | Total Units | Total Points | Avg Cost/Unit | Most Expensive Single Loss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Armored** | 74 | 14,440 | 195 | T1 Abrams (353) |
| **Mechanized** | 110 | 14,308 | 130 | T2A1 Bradley (271) |
| **Motorized** | 97 | 3,853 | 40 | TOW HMMWV (171) |
| **Support** | 61 | 6,316 | 104 | T109A6 Paladin (234) |
| **Droptroops** | 78 | 5,122 | 66 | AH-1F Cobra (430) |

### What This Means for Balance

- **Armored and Mechanized** cost ~14,000 points. Losing 10% of your force costs ~1,400 points to replace. That's *expensive* — roughly the cost of an entire Motorized Rifle Company.
- **Motorized** costs ~3,800 points. Losing 10% costs ~385 points. You can absorb punishment that would wreck an Armored player's campaign. But you can't kill a Siege Walker.
- **Support** cannot survive alone. The security platoon is a speed bump. But the 12 Paladins behind it can delete anything on the map from 10km away.
- **Droptroops** live and die by their helicopters. Lose the Blackhawks and you lose insertion capability. Lose the Cobras and you lose anti-armor. The infantry is cheap; the aviation is not.

## How Rosters Change Over Time

See REPLACEMENT_AND_REINFORCEMENT.md for the full replacement system. In summary:

- **Destroyed units** are gone until replaced with supply points
- **Replacement options** offer like-for-like or cheaper alternatives (downgrade ladder)
- **Upgrades** are earned through campaign milestones (liberating key planets, completing special missions)
- **No unit type can be replaced with a type outside your battalion's access list** — an Armored player can never field Paladins, a Support player can never field MBTs

The starting roster is the peak of what you begin with. Attrition pulls you down the ladder. Campaign progress pushes you back up — potentially past your starting point.
